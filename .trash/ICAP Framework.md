The framework proposes that behaviour can be categorised in 4 moods.

![[Pasted image 20250211094954.png#invert]]
It predicts that as you move down the 4 moods, more interactive, your learning success will increase.

## Passive
Learners being orientated towards receiving information from the instrumental materials without doing nay else related to learning

Like listening to lecture/videos without making notes

## Active
Active tasks have shown to exceeds that of passive tasks, it includes doing things like underlining and highlighting important sentences within notes. 

This is better than just reading the notes.
## Constructive
This is defined as learners generating additional outputs or products beyond what was provided in the learning materials.

This includes activities like drawing mind maps, asking questions and making notes in your own words.
## Interactive
This is defined as interaction with a device, a learning environment or system.

It requires each person of the group to interact constructively.
E.g. if students copy each others homework this would be classed as active and not constructive

So this can be classed as either discourse or dialoguing. 
Discourse refers to verbal interaction
Not all discourse is interactive

Methods of interactive activities:
- Jigsaw discussion
- Peer review
- Group Projects
