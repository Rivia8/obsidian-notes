# Classical Statistics

This refers to the traditional approach to statistics, which focuses on analysing data using fixed models and probabilities derived from long-run frequencies.


## Hypothesis

Statistics is about hypothesis testing data.

A hypothesis is a statement that can either be true or false, a valid scientific hypothesis must be **disprovable**, **testable** and **falsifiable** (there must be an experiment that proves it wrong).

Empirical science can never "prove" a hypothesis true, its based on **induction**: drawing general conclusions from specific observations.

*i.e. "all swans are white" after seeing 10,000 swans it may be true but then you find one black swan in Australia it ultimately falsifies it.*

This is why you can't *prove* a hypothesis is true, but you can **falsify** it.
But it doesn't just take one attempt (in most cases) but rather a they **withstand** attempts to disprove them.
Even the best experiments have some degree of **measurement error,** **model assumptions,** etc.
That is why when we perform [[Hypothesis Testing|hypothesis testing]] we have a **confidence level** (the *complement* of the *significance level*).
