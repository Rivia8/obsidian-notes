# Bayesian Principles

**Bayesian Principles** revolve around a different way of thinking compared to that of [[Classical Statistics|classical statistics]].

For a **Frequentist view**: probability is the proportion of times an event would occur in a large number of repeated trials. For example, the probability of a coin landing on heads is 0.5, because if you flip it many times it will land on heads half the time.
In the **Bayesian view:** Probability reflects our uncertainty or degree about belief about an event. 

Bayes way of going about probability is seen as 'subjective'.

## Subjective Vs Objective probability.

**Objective** **probability** is seen as property of the world itself and is independent of any individual's belief or knowledge. It represents the inherent likelihood of an event occurring based on the physical properties of the system or the long-run frequency of repeatable events.
There are different angles when looking at objective probability: the **frequentist** interpretation and the **propensity** interpretation.

**Subjective probability** (also often called "degree of belief") is a measure of an individual's confidence or belief that an event will occur. It reflects their state of knowledge or uncertainty.

---

When it comes to looking specifically at **Bayes Interpretation** a key aspect within the methods and the considerations you take is the **prior beliefs,** $P(H)$.
This is what you learn from past experience and as new evidence arrives you **update** the beliefs into posterior beliefs, $P(H|E)$. 

This is called subjective because the prior probability originates from an individual's judgement, experience or existing knowledge. 

There is nuance, even though that Bayesian statistics is considered subjective it doesn't mean anything goes:
Bayesian probabilities must adhere to the axioms of probability, ensuring internal consistencies.
