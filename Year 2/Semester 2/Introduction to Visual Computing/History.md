## History

### 1920 - Bartlane Picture Transmission Service

Invented by Mr. Harry G. Bartholomew and Captain Maynard D. McFarlane, both of the Daily Mirror, United Kingdom.

They produced a transmitter that had 5 grey levels possible from light shining though holes, allows the transmission of images.

This was then upgrade to a higher level system (more levels of gray).

### 1964 - Computer Enhancement of Images

We had computer enhancement of images from the NASA Ranger 7 moon probe.

### 1979 -  Computer Assisted Topography

Invented by Allan M. Cormack, South Africa and Godfrey N. Hounsfield, United Kingdom.

Created a CT machine.

## Applications

When it comes to creating images there are different data sources that can be used to produce said images.

- Electromagnetic
	- Gamma rays
	- X-Rays
	- Visible
	- Microwave
	- Radiowave
- Others:
	- Soundwaves
	- Magnetic Fields


CAT (Computer Axial Tomography) or CT (Computed Tomography) scans they use electromagnetic waves 

X-Ray Scans, use X-Rays hence their name.

Gamma-rays are utilised too with radio-pharmaceuticals. These are taken internally and emit gamma rays, radioactive material is absorbed by the body and the emitted gamma rays are detected and imaged.

Gamma rays are also in astronomy.

Ultraviolet rays are used too for things like detecting fingerprints

Infrared can detect heat.

Microwaves are used for radio detection and ranging, they map out areas of the ground by measuring the time taken for the microwave to reach back to the emitter.

Lidar (Light Detection and Ranging), this is similar to radio detection and ranging but uses lasers instead of microwaves.

Ultrasound uses a similar type of method, this is used for imaging a pregnant ladies' baby. From ultrasounds we can also render the images to produce 3D renders.

Sonar is similar to ultrasounds, this transmits soundwaves to objects under the sea and measures the time taken for the reflection of the sonar to come back.

**Reflection Seismology** is when we use image processing to find what's under the ground. You produce vibrations at different points and map concentric circles to find whereabouts an object is under the ground. 
This is commonly used for oil exploration.

**Astronomy** has lots of uses for image processing. One way they use image processing is taking lots of images of the same celestial object with different wavelengths of light, and the collating the images together to produce a final image.

**Weather** can be imaged using infrared.

**Agriculture** this is more Computer Vision, but robots are used to for automated pruning of vines, the robots are able to detect the vines.

Within the policing and security sector lots of imaging is used. For an example, automatic number plate detection.
Ultraviolet is used to view things like footprints on waxed floors.

Remote sensing within satellite imagery. 
**Synthetic Aperture Radar** **(SAR)** is used for monitoring marine traffic.

In geography, there are lots of applications. One specific one is Coastal Erosion. To do this pictures of the same coast are taken at different points of time, and they are aligned using **Image Registration** by using anchor points (like houses in the images - something that hasn't changed).

In **Archaeology** image processing is used. In 2015 the remains of 90 standing stones were discovered at Stonehenge using aerial imaging.

The **Beauty Industry** heavily uses image processing with things like filters.

Snapchat's filters use things like selective warping.
